About

Researcher, Lecturer & Policy Advisor

Dr Edmund Terem Ugar is a researcher, lecturer and policy advisor working at the intersection of applied ethics, artificial intelligence governance, healthcare AI and African philosophy.

His work explores how artificial intelligence can be designed, governed and deployed in ways that advance justice, human dignity, cultural relevance and responsible innovation, particularly within African contexts.

He holds a PhD in Philosophy from the University of Johannesburg. His doctoral research examined discrimination and exploitation in medical machine-learning technologies, using African philosophical tools to support more ethical and inclusive AI systems.

Research Areas

  • AI Ethics and Governance
  • AI Policy and Regulation
  • Healthcare AI and Medical Ethics
  • Generative AI and Large Language Models
  • Big Data and Algorithmic Bias
  • African Philosophy of Technology
  • Responsible Innovation and Digital Justice

Current Roles

  • Research Supervisor — Postgraduate Diploma in Artificial Intelligence, MANCOSA
  • AI Ethics and Governance Fellow — Policy Innovation Centre, Abuja
  • Africa AI Policy Fellow — Lawyers Hub & AI Policy Lab
