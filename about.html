About Dr Edmund Terem Ugar

Researcher • Lecturer • Policy Advisor

Dr Edmund Terem Ugar is a researcher, lecturer and policy advisor specialising in AI Ethics, AI Governance, African Relational Ethics, healthcare technologies and responsible artificial intelligence. His work focuses on developing ethical, inclusive and culturally responsive AI systems that address real-world challenges across Africa and beyond.

His research explores how artificial intelligence can be governed and deployed in ways that promote justice, human dignity, accountability and responsible innovation. By integrating African relational ethics into AI governance, his work contributes to creating technologies that are socially beneficial, culturally grounded and ethically responsible.

Dr Ugar obtained his PhD in Philosophy from the University of Johannesburg. His doctoral research investigated discrimination and exploitation in medical machine-learning technologies, applying African ethical frameworks to advance fairness, transparency and responsible AI in healthcare.

Mission

Dr Ugar's mission is to bridge philosophy, public policy and emerging technologies by developing ethical AI systems that strengthen human flourishing, social justice and sustainable innovation across Africa.
